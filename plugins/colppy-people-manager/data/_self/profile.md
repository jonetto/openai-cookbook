## Feedback from my CEO / Coaching Program

_Source: "Coaching Juan Onetto Work Plan 4Q 2025" — Coaches: Matt + Jeanine_
_Period: Oct–Dec 2025, 8 weekly sessions of 60–90min_

### What the coaching program identified

**The core challenge:** Juan needs to evolve from "Start-Up CEO" to "Scale-Up CEO." The behaviors that enabled Colppy's survival are now blocking its next phase.

**Matt's direct observations:**

- Juan is frustrated at Colppy's performance. He often directs that frustration at his direct reports. Rather than inspiring them to raise their games, this dynamic can lead them to disengage.
- This dynamic can also lead Juan to "move down" one level and micro-manage his team — or in some cases, do their jobs for them.
- It has taken longer than expected to rebuild a senior management team committed to a high-performance culture.

**Coaching objectives defined:**

1. Identify what Colppy expects and needs from its CEO today
2. Evaluate Juan's self-perceived strengths and gaps vs. role expectations
3. Establish strengths and gaps as perceived by teammates (Direct Reports, PBP, CEO SUMA, SUMA Regional Team)
4. Achieve recognition of improvement by teammates in the identified development areas
5. Deliver Colppy's 4Q 2025 and 1Q 2026 targets
6. Improve the climate of the Colppy manager team

### Start-Up vs. Scale-Up CEO — the transformation model

| Skill to Unlearn (Start-Up Mode) | Why It Becomes a Liability | Skill to Adopt (Scale-Up Mode) |
| --- | --- | --- |
| "I'll do it myself" / individual execution | Caps growth at CEO's personal capacity | Delegation and empowerment |
| Micromanagement & direct involvement | Disempowers leaders, creates bottleneck | Strategic oversight and guidance |
| Decisions based on intuition/gut | Unreliable as complexity grows | Data-driven decision-making |
| Tactical focus (putting out fires) | Prevents working on the future | Proactive system-building |
| Focus on product over market/processes | Neglects operational and market expansion | Market domination strategy + ops optimization |
| Fear of letting go / control | Leads to burnout, poor retention | Trust and accountability |

The core reframe: move from being the primary "doer" and "individual hero" to becoming an "organizational architect" and "system builder."

## My leadership history

**Context:** I returned to Colppy as CEO after a period away. In my first year back:

- I quickly dismantled the existing senior management team and the prevailing "comfort" culture.
- I began rebuilding toward a high-performance standard — but rebuilding the team has taken longer than expected (12+ months), which has forced me to operate below my level.
- Colppy's commercial and operating results have been below budget and expectations — including some metrics below prior-year performance.
- The experience of running a startup shaped me as a decisive, fast-moving, results-driven leader. That muscle is real. The liability is that I continue to lead the same way as the company grows into needing a different kind of CEO.

**What I know about myself:**

- I am genuinely committed to Colppy's success. My drive is real, not performative.
- I think in big pictures about the business and can read the market well.
- I am open to feedback when it is delivered with data and context — I can update my position.
- I tend to get anxious when results are below expectations, and that anxiety leads me to go down into operations rather than up into strategy.
- My emotional impact on others is a blind spot. What I experience as urgency, others experience as pressure. What I experience as transparency, others experience as overloading short messages.

**What I'm working on long-term:**

- Learning to lead through others rather than through myself.
- Building the team around me that Colppy needs for scale — not just replacing individuals, but building a system.
- Developing my ability to differentiate coaching from accountability (I tend to default to accountability first).
- Growing my awareness of how I land emotionally with each person — especially under pressure.

## Active Development Themes

Synthesized from 360 + coaching program — these are the through-lines to track across sessions.

1. **Delegation and trust** — Stop resolving things myself when results disappoint. Build systems that enable the team to own execution. The bottleneck is me.

2. **Emotional impact vs. emotional intent** — My intent when I'm direct/urgent is to drive results. My impact is often disengagement, anxiety, or feeling unheard. Learn to separate what I need from what lands well for each person.

3. **Talent development as a discipline** — Individual development plans, structured feedback, intentional coaching conversations. This is the lowest-rated competency and the one I am weakest at building into my routine.

4. **Team culture vs. team accountability** — I create accountability well. I don't create the psychological safety that makes accountability sustainable. Both are required.

5. **Strategic vs. operational presence** — When performance is off, I go down into operations. This is the pattern that needs to break. Stay at the balcony even when the floor is burning.
