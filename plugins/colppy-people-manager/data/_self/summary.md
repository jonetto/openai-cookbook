## My Leadership Profile

**What's confirmed across sessions:** I drive accountability by assigning public ownership, naming standards, and holding the line under pressure. I can facilitate high-stakes confrontations with directness that stays productive — naming patterns ("cada vez que reaccionás así, me vas a tener mucho más alejado"), drawing hard lines ("no existen más planes de seis meses"), and redirecting intensity instincts toward structure (stopped the "12 hours in a room" idea).

**What's also confirmed:** I default to tightening structure when results are off-track. This works, but I stay in the wrong format too long. On March 9, the confrontation data had been available for weeks — I'd been raising the same concerns in group meetings where Ale deflected each time. The escalation from group challenge to individual confrontation should have happened 3-4 weeks earlier. Format matters as much as content.

## My Execution Patterns

- High-structure approach under pressure: weekly presenter roles, MRR baselines, migration deadlines, explicit accountability assignments.
- Evidence-based confrontation model (March 9): brought Malén with independent People data from team 1:1s. Made the feedback undeniable. This is a better instrument than arguing from my own observations alone.
- Can hold multiple business crises in a single session (NPS, MRR, migration, people departures) without losing the thread.
- Risk: I go deep into operations when I lose trust in a leader's reporting. On March 9, I knew more about individual engineer 1:1 schedules than Ale did. That's a signal I've gone too deep.
