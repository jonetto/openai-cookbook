## Personality and Working Style

**From direct observation across Fellow meetings, calibration sessions, Granola feedforward transcript, and CEO coaching context:**

- **Brings benchmarks from previous experience.** Frequently references Tienda Nube and Intercom models for prioritization, ICP segmentation, and support scaling. This grounds her proposals in real-world evidence, not theory.
- **Iterative collaborator on plans.** Built the Customer 2026 plan involving her team and involving Juan in document reviews. This is collaborative, not top-down.
- **Pushes for higher standards in others.** In calibrations, she was the one who challenged whether senior behaviors were truly "alta alineación" or just expected baseline. This is a leadership signal.
- **Balances exigencia with cercanía deliberately.** "Yo soy súper exigente... pero a su vez no perder la cercanía con las personas." Conducts quarterly check-ins with 4 dimensions. Lucas told her he'd never had a leader who felt close. She sees this balance as requiring intentional time investment.
- **Deliberate working method — 2-week sprints.** Goes deep into one priority at a time for ~2 weeks, then rotates. "Yo priorizo... si vos le preguntas a Maga, en cuánto detalle estoy de Kea, estoy en todo." This is intentional, not scattered.
- **Tendency to solve alone.** When she sees gaps in other areas, she jumps in rather than engaging peers. "Mucha tendencia a resolver vos." Risk of becoming a silo. She acknowledged this in feedforward but behavioral change not yet visible.
- **Self-aware about AI limitations.** Explicitly said: "Adapto la inteligencia artificial al paradigma anterior... No tengo ese mindset hoy." Asked for help rather than deflecting.
- **Protective of personal boundaries early.** The Thursday presencialidad refusal came very early in her tenure, before establishing political capital. This created team friction and signals either a genuine hard constraint or insufficient reading of organizational context.
- **Action-oriented onboarding.** The jacatón was a strong move — she didn't wait for permission or process; she organized a day and did the work with the team. This earned credibility.
- **Honest upward communicator.** In feedforward, she told Juan directly that his problem is packaging/communicating his vision — "Siento que tenés mucho para aportar y el problema es que no sabés cómo hacerlo." This level of honest upward feedback at 3 months is notable.
- **Strong peer relationship with Malén.** "Puedo tener conversaciones muy honestas con ella y no me cuido tanto." This signals a trusted alliance forming in the leadership team.
