## Background

Agustin comes from e-commerce — most recently responsible for marketing at Buque Bus. Hired to unify marketing and sales under a single Revenue function, replacing the previous head of sales role. The organizational restructuring was announced December 2, 2025 (Fellow note_id=62914977), with Agustin starting the following week.

The restructuring moved the canal de contadores (fidelización) from sales to Customer (under Agostina), leaving Agustin focused on **growth**: demand generation, marketing, and new sales. This was a deliberate design decision — the previous structure created friction where salespeople had to manage both new business and existing contador relationships.

Agustin was deeply involved in the 2026 planning from day one — participating in financial modeling (Building Blocks), defining ICP distribution, ticket averages, and Q1 forecasting during his first two weeks. He was present at the December 17 planning wrap-up (virtually), December 19 presencial closure, December 23 MRR referido session, and January 2 virtual check.

His onboarding was impacted by: (1) two weeks of vacation during the holiday period, (2) Juan's own admission that he "inhibited" the setup of weekly 1:1s due to an assumption Agustin wouldn't need hand-holding, and (3) the complexity of inheriting a team described as "cosa morfa" (a mess) with performance issues.

## Revenue Plan

_Source: [Google Doc](https://docs.google.com/document/d/1SxplYdMQor1pjdZhYclUd0nVb6Qd6Zp5Unn2EqNI00Q/edit)_

**Core strategic direction:** Transform SME management through technological innovation. Three primary segments: small businesses, accounting firms, payroll services. Current penetration ~0.2% of ~600K Argentine SMEs; target 3,000–5,000 clients by 2026 (~1% TAM).

**Five strategic "Big Bets":**
1. Intelligent cash management using comprehensive platform data
2. Connect SMEs with accounting and administration expert ecosystems
3. Maximize rapid adoption velocities
4. Ecosystem connectivity initiatives

**2026 revenue objectives:**
- Target new clients: 3,000–5,000 (from ~2,000 current base)
- Budget allocation: progressive quarterly increases ($50.8M Q1 → $56.1M Q4)
- Diverse income streams through service improvements, digital marketing, and strategic partnerships

**Customer segmentation:**
- SME ICP: 20+ monthly transactions, $100K+ annual billing, need organizational clarity on profitability
- Accounting Firm ICP: 10+ SME clients, provide admin/tax/payroll services, software responsibility for client offerings

**Acquisition strategies:**
- Paid media (Google, Meta, LinkedIn), inbound/SEO, direct sales (HIGH TOUCH for SMEs), partner/referral (LOW TOUCH for accountants), outbound prospecting (limited Q1)
- Quality emphasis: "fewer leads that actually convert, pay better and last"

**Retention & expansion:** Churn target <1.5% monthly, NRR ≥105%, cross-sell payroll module, differentiated onboarding by customer type.

**Key 2026 metrics:** CAC payback ≤18 months, LTV/CAC ratio 4x, win-rate targets for direct sales, MQL quality improvements across channels.
