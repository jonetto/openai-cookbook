# Alejandro Soto — Coaching Arc

_Last updated: 2026-03-09_

## Development trajectory

**First observation: May 2025.** Joined after a 6-month search as Head of Product Engineering — the highest-scope hire on Juan's direct team. Engineering veteran (8 years at La Nación), product management a new muscle.

**May-June 2025:** The guardias debate within days of joining revealed the core tension that would define his tenure: structured systems thinking (La Nación) vs. Colppy's organic ownership culture. He apologized and aligned in behavior, but the underlying belief may have persisted. The ARCA outage followed weeks later — crisis response present, coordination absent. "I'm not their boss" was the telling phrase.

**Q3 2025:** Planning challenged as vague and maintenance-heavy. Juan recognized early AI adoption (Gemini for user stories, AI in PR reviews) — a genuine win. But connecting engineering decisions to commercial outcomes remained underdeveloped.

**Q4 2025:** Did not meet any proposed Q4 goals. Planning remained unstructured. Leadership matrix: partial alignment (2/4 behaviors) across all five SUMA competencies. Widest gap: Inspira Talento (self-rates SUMA Standard, Juan rates En Desarrollo, 8/9 behaviors FALSE).

**Feb 2026 — Feedforward cycle:** Two sessions in 11 days. Juan's feedforward to Ale (Feb 2, 57 minutes in-person): named every problem that would surface in March — migration timeline, communication defensiveness ("cada vez que reaccionás así, me vas a tener mucho más alejado"), team autonomy gaps, PM gap as "talón de Aquiles." Ale's upward feedback to Juan (Feb 13): raised format changes, unclear ownership, and resistance to cross-functional decisions. Both sessions productive on the surface. But no lightweight checkpoint existed between Feb 2 and March 9 — 37 days elapsed with no observable behavior change.

**March 2-8:** Engineer 1:1s surfaced team damage: Federico (flight risk, zero business context reaching ICs), Ignacio (migration metrics hypothetical for 1.5 months, better ideas publicly suppressed), Mario (evaluating exit in 3 months, chatbot story as workaround culture codified). Three-audience problem documented in planning artifacts. Exit decision made by Juan + Malén (2026-03-06). Alejandro does not know.

**March 9 (confrontation):** Escenario B with active resistance. Committed to three actions under pressure. Relationship has shifted from development to managed transition.

## Coaching approaches tried

| Approach | When | What happened |
|---|---|---|
| Direct verbal feedback (Slack + in-person) | Throughout 2025 | Adapted behavior momentarily; underlying beliefs persisted |
| SUMA matrix evaluation | Mid 2025 | Accepted the evaluation; widest gap (Inspira Talento) not challenged |
| Structured feedforward (57 min, in-person) | Feb 2, 2026 | Named every core issue; no observable behavior change in 37 days |
| Evidence-based confrontation (Juan + Malén) | March 9, 2026 | Escenario B — commitments made under direct pressure |
| Engineer data (Federico, Ignacio, Mario) | March 2026 | Changed risk calculus; shifted framing from "develop faster" to "transition responsibly" |

## Breakthrough moments

**Feb 13 upward feedback session:** Alejandro gave Juan direct upward feedback about format changes and unclear ownership. Shows he can use feedback as a tool when he's the giver — the mechanism works in one direction.

**March 9 commitments:** Under pressure and in the presence of People data, Alejandro did not deny all facts and committed to actions. This is the clearest evidence that direct confrontation with data moves him. Too late for the development relationship, but diagnostic for what would have worked earlier: not gentle nudges, but clear data + explicit expectations + a co-facilitator with independent evidence.

## Regression patterns

**"Not my area" under pressure:** Surfaced first in June 2025 ("I'm not their boss"), then in the Feb 13 feedforward ("won't subsidize other areas"), confirmed in March 9 ("we already did our part," defending that migration is on track). Each time Juan pressed cross-functional ownership, Ale drew a boundary.

**Migration timeline rigidity:** Juan challenged the 6-month scope in February. Ale maintained it through March 9 until an explicit line was drawn ("no existen más planes de seis meses"). The structural belief — that scope negotiation is the CEO's job, not the leader's — persisted across multiple interventions.

**Defensive communication pattern:** When confronted with negative data, first response is explanation and context, not acknowledgment. True in the ARCA outage conversation, the Feb feedforward, and the March 9 meeting. Shifts only when pressure is maintained. The communication gap named in February is the same gap that accelerated the exit decision in March.

## Evolution of this coaching relationship

**Phase 1 — Active development (May–Dec 2025):** Oriented toward developing him into the full Head of Product Engineering role. Multiple honest conversations, recognition of genuine wins (security, AI adoption), patience with the learning curve on product thinking.

**Phase 2 — Formal evaluation (Jan–Feb 2026):** The feedforward cycle formalized what had been building: 0/5 on bias check, 2/4 behaviors on every competency. The Feb 2 session was the last genuine attempt to give him a clear picture and a path forward.

**Phase 3 — Evidence phase (March 1–8, 2026):** Engineer conversations made the team damage visible. Importantly, all three direct conversations (Mario lunch Feb 24, Federico DM, Ignacio DM) were with Juan directly — not filtered through Malén. Juan also has a ~6-month cycle of direct meetings with all Colppy employees, so his visibility into the team was his own. Malén contributed the People perspective and co-facilitation credibility for the confrontation. But the decision was accelerated by a convergence of Juan's own signals, not by dependency on a single People function.

**Phase 4 — Managed transition (March 9 onward):** The coaching question is no longer about development. It's about: (1) holding space for him to execute transition commitments while the Tech Lead search proceeds, (2) protecting the engineering team from departure, and (3) ensuring Juan's direct engagement with the team during the bridge doesn't create new dependency patterns.

**What this case teaches for the next hire:** Juan had multiple visibility layers throughout — group meetings, direct engineer conversations, his own ~6-month cycle with all Colppy employees. The gap wasn't visibility. It was the escalation threshold: the decision rule for when to move from "group challenge" (where Ale could manage his response publicly) to "individual confrontation" (where he couldn't). The combination that finally triggered escalation was: repeated public denial in group meetings + three independent engineer signals + planning artifact analysis converging simultaneously. The question for the next hire: what's the minimum signal set that should trigger moving formats, earlier?
