### Onboarding (~May 2025)

Juan's initial assessment: "Alejandro entered after a 6-month search. He is the new responsible for Product Engineering. A very important addition to the company with very high expectations. His strength is engineering, not product — there's a lot for him to learn. He's the most senior and with the most responsibility on my direct team."

### Wizard Deploy Conversation (~May 2025)

First significant cultural friction. Nate reported the wizard was delayed because Alejandro found major auth security flaws — a genuine technical win. But the conversation about deployment availability exposed a deep values gap:

- **Alejandro proposed** "guardias pasivas" (paid on-call shifts) for 24/7 coverage
- **Juan firmly rejected** the concept: "My position is no guardias. No economic incentive to work beyond what we already have as commitment."
- **Key exchange:** Alejandro said "your case can't be taken as reference, you founded the company — does anyone else at Colppy have that level of involvement?" Juan responded: "I knew it had to do with how much people take ownership."
- **Nate intervened** to de-escalate: "Let's leave this here, seems a bit counterproductive."
- **Alejandro closed:** "My apologies, it wasn't the moment or place to discuss this. I'm going to put everything within my reach so Colppy gets better every day."

**Signal:** Alejandro showed professional maturity by apologizing and aligning, but the underlying belief difference (structured ops processes vs. organic ownership culture) remains unresolved. He adapted his *behavior* without necessarily changing his *belief*.

### Early Wins (~May-June 2025)

- Started incorporating AI: Gemini in user story definitions, AI in pull request reviews
- Learning to navigate cross-functional priority requests (e.g., when to prioritize HubSpot work for Marketing vs. core product)
- Juan acknowledged: "He's trying to incorporate all these things. That should be recognized."

### ARCA Outage (June 19, 2025)

Service went down completely — customers couldn't issue electronic invoices. Alejandro was working and attentive, but:

- Did not effectively coordinate the War Room
- Team disconnected without guaranteeing containment
- Juan requested a War Room connection, people connected then disconnected
- Result: instability in customer support, cases kept arriving with no one coordinating the real-time response

**Key gap exposed:** Being present and working is not the same as leading the incident response. Alejandro was *doing* but not *coordinating*.

### Post-Crisis Confrontation (June 19 evening)

Alejandro directly messaged Juan: "I'd like to know if there's a specific problem with me — I see all the criticism directed at me."

This conversation is a critical behavioral artifact:

- **Alejandro's valid points:** He was the only one responding. The criticism felt personal because he was the only one there. He asked what more he could do to help. He offered to take action (turn off the broken flow).
- **Alejandro's growth edge:** He said "I'm not their boss, I'm not the one to demand from them" — revealing he doesn't yet see cross-functional leadership as part of his role.
- **Juan's response:** "You're not their boss, you're their coworker. And your work depends on them — even more than on me."
- **Alejandro's closing move:** "Let me talk to Dami to see what she prefers" — moved to action despite the tension.

**Pattern revealed:** Under pressure, Alejandro defaults to "I can only control my own area" rather than "I need to lead the coordination regardless of org chart." This connects directly to the matrix gap in "Multiplica el Impacto" — he collaborates but doesn't yet lead laterally.

## Development Themes

Synthesized from leadership matrix, Slack conversations, crisis observations, and coaching notes.

1. **From responding to coordinating** — He shows up during crises, but leads as an individual contributor not as the incident commander. The shift needed: own the coordination, not just the execution. When things break, he should be the one pulling people together, not waiting for Juan to do it.

2. **Cross-functional influence without authority** — He said "I'm not their boss" during the June 19 incident. True technically, but his role requires leading peers (Dami, Carla, Marketing) through influence, data, and shared purpose — not through org chart authority. This is the "Multiplica el Impacto" gap in the matrix.

3. **Strategic product sense** — His instincts lean toward maintenance, stability, and engineering quality (all valid). The growth needed: connecting product decisions to commercial outcomes — MRR, activation, NPS recovery, sales velocity. He needs to think "what does the business need from product?" not just "what does the platform need from engineering."

4. **Cultural integration: ownership vs. process** — He comes from structured environments (La Nación, 8 years of guardias). Colppy's culture expects organic ownership without formal incentives. He's adapting his behavior but may not yet have internalized the belief. Watch for whether he truly shifts or just complies.

5. **Talent and team development** — The widest gap in the matrix (SUMA Standard self-rating vs. En Desarrollo from Juan, with 8/9 behaviors FALSE). He doesn't yet give structured feedback, create development plans, or use coaching as a leadership tool. This is a critical growth area as his team scales.

## Feedforward Session — Juan → Alejandro (Feb 2, 2026)

Source: Fellow recording, note_id=67974818.

### What Juan recognized

- **Availability during crises:** Alejandro was constantly present during 2025 operational problems and cloud service outages. When others made excuses, he showed up with extra effort. Juan wants him to maintain this professional spirit.

### What Juan asked him to develop

1. **Planning:** Identified as a skill that isn't natural for Alejandro. Needs more "brain power" dedicated to it. Implement **weekly progressive iterations** instead of planning in large blocks. Break 6-month projects into 3-month, then 1-month, then weekly checkpoints.

2. **Communication:** Not just as a natural skill, but as _investment of time and effort_ in the act of communicating. Clarity comes from how numbers are written and presented. When context is missing, repetitive questions arise. Provide data and evidence behind decisions regardless of format.

3. **Team autonomy:** Current approach may be too much "ticket handoff." Instead, don't give everything solved — let the team think and propose. Create roundtable spaces where you ask "what occurred to you?" Foster better reception of team ideas — some team members preemptively expect rejection ("he'll tell me it's wrong").

4. **Meeting dynamics:** Design the relationship between team members through meetings. Create spaces for exchange, not just reporting. Make meetings have _meaning_ by connecting problems to collaborative solutions.

### Structural issue identified

**Missing a PM manager on the team** — described as the "Achilles heel." This forces Alejandro to do negotiation and management tasks that consume time and prevent strategic work. Product area problems have led to excessive detail involvement in operational matters.
