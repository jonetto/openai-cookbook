_Last updated: 2026-03-02_

## Leadership Profile

**What's confirmed across multiple data points:** Alejandro is committed, available, and technically strong. During every crisis in his first year, he was the one who showed up. He identified auth security flaws the team missed. He's incorporating AI into workflows (Gemini for user stories, AI in PR reviews). His drive is genuine — not performative.

**What's also confirmed:** He operates as an individual contributor in a leadership role. He responds but doesn't coordinate. He executes but doesn't plan with structure. He collaborates but doesn't lead cross-functionally. He evaluates others' gaps accurately but doesn't yet apply the same lens to his own behavior.

**Feedforward result (Feb 2026):** 0/5 on bias validation questions. Did not meet any proposed Q4 goals. All five SUMA competencies showed 2/4 behaviors observed (partial alignment). Strongest in crisis response and technical quality; weakest in planning, strategic communication, and proactive information sharing.

## Execution Profile

**Strengths:** Technical quality of fixes (no platform degradation), security response, availability during incidents, willingness to learn product, seeking feedback for improvement.

**Gaps:** Planning and project structure (2025 plan never took clear form, scattered across Wikis with no follow-up), NPS report delivered late and too basic for decision-making, information not shared proactively, Q4 goals unmet.

**Structural constraint:** Missing a PM manager on his team — identified as "Achilles heel" in feedforward session. This forces him into operational detail that prevents strategic work.

## Active Coaching Themes

Synthesized from leadership matrix, feedforward evaluation, feedforward sessions, calibrations, Slack conversations, and group observations.

1. **From responding to coordinating** — Shows up during crises but leads as IC not incident commander. The shift: own the coordination, pull people together, don't wait for Juan. Connected to ARCA outage (June 19) where he was working but not coordinating the War Room.

2. **Cross-functional influence without authority** — Said "I'm not their boss" during June 19 crisis. In feedforward to Juan (Feb 13), maintained position of not "subsidizing other areas' work." His role requires leading peers through influence and data, not org chart authority. This is the core "Multiplica el Impacto" gap.

3. **Planning as a discipline** — Planning is not natural for him. Juan asked for weekly progressive iterations instead of large blocks. Break 6-month projects into shorter checkpoints. The 2025 plan scattered across Wikis is the clearest evidence. Feedforward: "sit and think and improve deliverable quality."

4. **Strategic communication** — Not just a presentation skill but an investment of time. Provide numbers, data, and context that justify decisions. When context is missing, repetitive questions arise. The NPS report example: requested multiple times, delivered late and too basic.

5. **Team culture and autonomy** — Current approach may be too much "ticket handoff." Juan's coaching: let team think and propose, create roundtable spaces, foster better reception of ideas. Some team members preemptively expect rejection ("he'll tell me it's wrong"). In calibrations, he accurately identified these same gaps in Carla and Jorge — but hasn't applied the diagnosis to himself.

6. **Cultural integration: ownership vs. process** — Comes from 8 years at La Nación with formal guardias. Colppy expects organic ownership. He's adapting behavior but may not have internalized the belief. The guardias debate (May 2025) was the first signal; the "not my area" reflex during crises is the ongoing one.

## My Coaching Approach

**What works with Alejandro:** Direct, data-based conversations. He can update his position when context and data are provided. He responds well to honest feedback and reciprocates it (gave upward feedback to Juan about format changes and ownership clarity). He builds connection through shared experience (asado, humor).

**What to watch for:** Compliance vs. genuine internalization. He adapts behavior in the moment but the underlying beliefs (guardias, "not my boss," area boundaries) may persist. Test by observing his behavior in the _next_ crisis — does he coordinate without being asked, or wait for Juan to pull people together?

**Coaching focus for next 1:1:** Planning discipline — review how the empresa administradas migration is being tracked. Is it in weekly iterations with visible checkpoints, or in a large block? This is the direct test of whether the feedforward coaching on planning is landing.
