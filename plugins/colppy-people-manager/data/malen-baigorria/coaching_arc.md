# Malén Baigorria — Coaching Arc
_Last updated: 2026-03-04_

## Development trajectory

**Mid-2025 (onboarding):** Arrived into organizational crisis (ARCA outage). Immediately demonstrated core capability — containing CEO reactivity, proposing structured responses, identifying "Juan-dependencia" as root cause. Positioned herself as strategic partner from day one, not waiting for permission.

**Q3-Q4 2025:** Built performance management infrastructure from scratch — calibration framework, leadership matrix, evaluation criteria. Established credibility as process authority. Early challenge: getting leaders to use performance tools as real management instruments rather than "administrative checks."

**Q1 2026:** Most complex quarter — simultaneous engineering retention crisis, sales restructuring, PM hiring exceptions, objectives audit (80% not SMART), culture/values rewrite, benefits overhaul, high-performance workshop. Managing volume while maintaining strategic altitude is the current test.

## Coaching approaches tried

- **Peer dialogue over directives:** Sharing thinking openly ("tengo acá algo que estuve procesando con Ale, a ver tu opinión") and asking for her perspective works well. She engages deeply when treated as strategic partner.
- **Challenging her visibility:** Pushed her to evolve Monday presentations from "novedades" to OKR-connected updates. She received this well ("le voy a dar una vuelta de rosca") but execution is pending verification.
- **Giving her strategic context:** Sharing business model thinking (product vision, PLG model, revenue model shifts) helps her connect People work to business outcomes. She engages substantively when given this context.
- **Structured bilateral feedback (feedforward):** The Mar 4 feedforward session — designed by Malén herself with 4 blocks — was the most productive coaching exchange to date. Both were vulnerable. Juan acknowledged her containment is valuable while asking her to redirect his energy. She named the recognition deficit. This format works: structured, prepared, peer-to-peer. She explicitly said she prefers this ("a mí me gusta enfocarme, sentarme, preparar").

## Breakthrough moments

- **ARCA crisis (June 2025):** Her response — "El problema no está en que se caiga la plataforma... me refiero a como todos reaccionan" — showed she sees organizational dynamics, not just incidents. This was the moment she established herself as more than traditional HR.
- **Calibration correction (Jan 15, 2026):** Caught that Juan's evaluation of Alejandro used incorrect methodology (didn't consider the calibration calculator, conflated quarterly evaluations). Pushed back directly and corrected the output. Demonstrated that she protects evaluation integrity even when the CEO is the one making the error.
- **Engineering retention early warning (Feb 27, 2026):** "Se van a ir todos" — she detected the signal through 1:1s before it became a crisis. Then orchestrated the response sequence: align with Juan first, then bring Alejandro in. Strategic, not reactive.
- **Feedforward vulnerability (Mar 4, 2026):** Named the recognition deficit she's been carrying: "He recibido mucha crítica tuya... no sé si estoy haciéndolo bien." Revealed that Juan's perceived distance and lack of positive feedback had caused her to retract — "me hizo retrotraerme un poco." Also named her own pattern: "soy muy de ir a resolver" rather than asking for help, which mirrors Juan's frustration that she doesn't bring topics to their 1:1 (90% initiated by him). This was the most vulnerable she's been in the relationship and explains the withdrawal pattern through a lens Juan hadn't considered: not disagreement, but accumulated feeling of not being valued.

## Regression patterns

- **Deferring to Rewards/SUMA:** When compensation topics arise (salary bands, exceptions), she defaults to "lo derivo a Rewards" rather than owning the Colppy recommendation. This may be a structural constraint (SUMA governance) or a comfort zone.
- **Self-doubt about strategic contribution:** "Siempre me queda gusto a poco" about her presentations. This surfaces periodically — she knows she can do better but hasn't fully bridged the gap between operational execution and strategic storytelling.
- **Process before speed tension:** She correctly holds process (aligning before acting) but this sometimes frustrates Juan's urgency. The product vision incident (Feb 24) showed this tension — she was right about process, but Juan felt blocked.
- **Containing without converting (core dynamic):** She consistently damps Juan's energy and urgency ("baja a tierra," "que no te gane la ansiedad," "ofuscándote no sirve") but doesn't redirect it into productive channels. She contains the CEO's anxiety without helping him install the standards he's trying to create. This creates a cycle: Juan pushes alone → she goes silent in the room → he pushes harder → she reads it as anxiety → she contains more. The net effect is the team learns to expect less rather than deliver more.
- **Withdrawal in group settings:** When dynamics they co-designed in 1:1 are deployed in the leadership team, she disengages — laptop, WhatsApp, or silent observation. She doesn't visibly back the shared plan, which leaves Juan pushing alone and the team reading misalignment.
- **Compliance escalation pattern:** When an employee came to her with concerns about being pushed (Slack messages after hours), she directed the person to file a formal compliance complaint rather than intervening earlier to mediate or coaching Juan to adjust. The instinct was to let the system handle it rather than contain the issue before it escalated.
- **Unilateral company-wide decisions (Mar 4, 2026):** When the office became unusable (fire/flood at cowork), she sent @channel telling everyone not to come in without consulting Juan first. His feedback: consult before such announcements; with short notice, logistics (alternative workspace) could have been explored. She agreed to consult first next time while holding her ground on substance (insurance, electric risk, safety). Positive: receptive to process feedback; did not become defensive.

## Evolution of this coaching relationship

**Initial phase (mid-2025):** Relationship established during crisis. She earned trust fast by being honest and useful simultaneously. The dynamic was set: she tells hard truths, he listens (mostly).

**Current phase (Q1 2026):** Relationship is maturing into genuine bilateral coaching. He coaches her on strategic visibility and business connection. She coaches him on patience, process, and emotional management. The feedforward exchange with Alejandro (where Juan shared his full analysis and asked "¿coincidís o en qué lo ves diferente?") represents the ideal mode of this relationship.

**Risk to watch:** She absorbs significant emotional load as the CEO's containment system. If organizational frustration intensifies (engineering crisis + sales underperformance + leadership gaps), her capacity to absorb while also executing may reach its limit.

**Central tension (identified Mar 3, named Mar 4):** Both Juan and Malén feel unsupported by the other, but in mirror-image ways. He feels she doesn't back him in the room when they've agreed on a direction. She revealed it's not disagreement but accumulated feeling of not being valued — "he recibido mucha crítica tuya" without corresponding recognition. This reframes the withdrawal pattern: she retracts not because she disagrees with the standard, but because she doesn't know if her contribution matters to him. The feedforward session named this explicitly. Both acknowledged it. The test is whether Juan's recognition changes her behavior in the room, and whether her new proactivity (bringing topics, co-owning delivery) changes his perception of support.

## Feedforward session outcomes (Mar 4, 2026)

**Session design:** 4 blocks as Malén designed. Both came prepared. Session ran ~60 min.

### Block 1 — Juan → Malén

**Three recognitions delivered:**
1. **Building frameworks:** Calibrations, objectives, "la vara" dynamics — "me da mucho orden, entiendo que tengo mis puntos ciegos, y eso ordena mucho las conversaciones con la gente." Superagradecido.
2. **Containment when he overflows:** "Cuando me desbordo, solés estar ahí." Values that she tells him what he needs to hear, not what he wants to hear. "Sos de las pocas personas que me decís, che, boludo, no."
3. **Co-creating together:** The moments they sit together to think about standards, expectations, dynamics — "cuando laburamos juntos y vibramos en esa energía, somos una masa." This is where he sees her greatest value.

**Four feedforward gifts delivered:**
1. **Back the standard in group settings:** When they've pre-worked a dynamic/standard and go to the team, she sometimes "afloja" — goes silent or disengages. Ask: "es más como pedirte que me acompañes." She received it.
2. **Redirect energy, not just contain it:** "¿Cómo me ayudás a recanalizar esa energía?" Not damping, but channeling. Open question, no prescribed solution.
3. **Early intervention on sensitive issues:** Referenced the compliance incident. His ask: come to him first next time so they can course-correct together. She explained she was bound by process (first time, complaint had more elements than just messages). He framed it as a gift: "la próxima vez que pase, inclusive no solo conmigo, sino con cualquier persona, cómo reaccionar frente a eso antes de ir por un camino muy poco constructivo."
4. **Stay present in strategic conversations:** She disengages (WhatsApp, phone, mentally checking out) when conversations go long or philosophical. He loses her support. Ask: find a way to signal when disconnecting rather than disappearing — "no dejarme solo." She acknowledged: "entiendo que se me nota mucho y que eso influye."

### Block 2 — Malén → Juan

**Her recognitions:**
1. **Real involvement:** Genuinely interested in everything happening in the company — not a distant CEO. "Podrías tranquilamente ser un CEO distante que se sienta en su oficina."
2. **Connecting all areas:** Sees the company end-to-end and connects business with people perspective. The culture deck he built himself — "no es algo que pase habitualmente."
3. **Genuine listening:** Doesn't just listen to confirm what he already thinks. "Generás otro forma de ver las cosas."
4. **Clarity when he decides:** When he sets priorities clearly, the team gains speed and focus. "La organización gana velocidad cuando vos hacés eso."

**Her feedforward gifts:**
1. **Cut philosophical conversations shorter:** Team gets confused about whether they're still exploring or a decision exists. "Dónde cortar la charla filosófica y decir, bueno, listo, acá llego."
2. **Be more explicit about expectations:** Do more of what he did with "la vara" document — state expectations upfront. When he doesn't, people work and then he says "no, yo quería esto." She said: "hacer más de eso."
3. **Lead by example on errors and learning:** He asks the team to recognize mistakes and learnings but doesn't do it publicly himself. "No te he escuchado tanto como reconocer errores y aprendizaje." He acknowledged this — linked it to his high self-demand and tendency to appear less fragile.
4. **Speak up in group settings:** In some recent sessions he stayed quiet to let leaders debate, but his company-wide vision is too important to withhold. "Tu voz se tiene que escuchar."

### Block 3 — How we work together

**Central revelation:** Malén named the recognition deficit: "He recibido mucha crítica tuya... no sé si estoy haciéndolo bien." She hasn't felt validated — today was the first time she heard explicit positive recognition from Juan. This caused her to retract: "me hizo retrotraerme un poco." She also named her own pattern: she doesn't ask for help ("soy muy de ir a resolver"), which was feedback she received before joining Colppy.

**Mirror insight:** She identified that 90% of topics in their 1:1s are initiated by Juan. She doesn't bring her own agenda. Both recognized this as a structural gap.

**Juan's vulnerability:** He acknowledged his autoexigencia is very high, and he masks angustia, tristeza, and miedo to avoid creating chaos. This is exactly what she asked for (leading by example on vulnerability).

**Structural agreements reached:**
1. Create a **People calendar** — annual roadmap of people processes (performance, culture, benefits timing), so they anticipate rather than react. She has it mentally; needs to formalize it.
2. **Restructure 1:1s** — separate operational (ongoing items) from strategic (anticipation, culture, people planning). Not burocratize, but create structure for both.
3. **She brings more topics** — explicitly commit to not leaving 90% of agenda to Juan.
4. **More co-work before deployment** — work together on deliverables (slides, frameworks, communications) before deploying to the team. Like they did with calibrations — but systematically.
5. **Define which topics must pass through both** — her list: leader decisions, evaluations/performance, role changes, tension situations, difficult conversations, org restructuring, sensitive cultural moments.
6. **Juan provides more positive recognition** — ongoing, not waiting for formal sessions.
7. **Juan states expectations explicitly** — more "vara" documents, clearer asks upfront.

**Her preferred feedback format:** Structured, prepared, peer-to-peer. Not informal Slack messages. "A mí me gusta enfocarme, sentarme, preparar." She wants ongoing feedback woven into their 1:1, not accumulated for big sessions.

**Compliance discussion:** The most tense moment. She held her ground on process ("me até al proceso por ser la primera vez") but acknowledged the relationship cost ("rompe la confianza"). He reframed as a gift rather than an accusation. Both navigated it without defensiveness. Net: she'll come to him first next time; he acknowledged the situation was more complex than he'd seen.
