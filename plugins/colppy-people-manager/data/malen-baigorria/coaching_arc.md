# Malén Baigorria — Coaching Arc
_Last updated: 2026-03-02_

## Development trajectory

**Mid-2025 (onboarding):** Arrived into organizational crisis (ARCA outage). Immediately demonstrated core capability — containing CEO reactivity, proposing structured responses, identifying "Juan-dependencia" as root cause. Positioned herself as strategic partner from day one, not waiting for permission.

**Q3-Q4 2025:** Built performance management infrastructure from scratch — calibration framework, leadership matrix, evaluation criteria. Established credibility as process authority. Early challenge: getting leaders to use performance tools as real management instruments rather than "administrative checks."

**Q1 2026:** Most complex quarter — simultaneous engineering retention crisis, sales restructuring, PM hiring exceptions, objectives audit (80% not SMART), culture/values rewrite, benefits overhaul, high-performance workshop. Managing volume while maintaining strategic altitude is the current test.

## Coaching approaches tried

- **Peer dialogue over directives:** Sharing thinking openly ("tengo acá algo que estuve procesando con Ale, a ver tu opinión") and asking for her perspective works well. She engages deeply when treated as strategic partner.
- **Challenging her visibility:** Pushed her to evolve Monday presentations from "novedades" to OKR-connected updates. She received this well ("le voy a dar una vuelta de rosca") but execution is pending verification.
- **Giving her strategic context:** Sharing business model thinking (product vision, PLG model, revenue model shifts) helps her connect People work to business outcomes. She engages substantively when given this context.

## Breakthrough moments

- **ARCA crisis (June 2025):** Her response — "El problema no está en que se caiga la plataforma... me refiero a como todos reaccionan" — showed she sees organizational dynamics, not just incidents. This was the moment she established herself as more than traditional HR.
- **Calibration correction (Jan 15, 2026):** Caught that Juan's evaluation of Alejandro used incorrect methodology (didn't consider the calibration calculator, conflated quarterly evaluations). Pushed back directly and corrected the output. Demonstrated that she protects evaluation integrity even when the CEO is the one making the error.
- **Engineering retention early warning (Feb 27, 2026):** "Se van a ir todos" — she detected the signal through 1:1s before it became a crisis. Then orchestrated the response sequence: align with Juan first, then bring Alejandro in. Strategic, not reactive.

## Regression patterns

- **Deferring to Rewards/SUMA:** When compensation topics arise (salary bands, exceptions), she defaults to "lo derivo a Rewards" rather than owning the Colppy recommendation. This may be a structural constraint (SUMA governance) or a comfort zone.
- **Self-doubt about strategic contribution:** "Siempre me queda gusto a poco" about her presentations. This surfaces periodically — she knows she can do better but hasn't fully bridged the gap between operational execution and strategic storytelling.
- **Process before speed tension:** She correctly holds process (aligning before acting) but this sometimes frustrates Juan's urgency. The product vision incident (Feb 24) showed this tension — she was right about process, but Juan felt blocked.
- **Containing without converting (core dynamic):** She consistently damps Juan's energy and urgency ("baja a tierra," "que no te gane la ansiedad," "ofuscándote no sirve") but doesn't redirect it into productive channels. She contains the CEO's anxiety without helping him install the standards he's trying to create. This creates a cycle: Juan pushes alone → she goes silent in the room → he pushes harder → she reads it as anxiety → she contains more. The net effect is the team learns to expect less rather than deliver more.
- **Withdrawal in group settings:** When dynamics they co-designed in 1:1 are deployed in the leadership team, she disengages — laptop, WhatsApp, or silent observation. She doesn't visibly back the shared plan, which leaves Juan pushing alone and the team reading misalignment.
- **Compliance escalation pattern:** When an employee came to her with concerns about being pushed (Slack messages after hours), she directed the person to file a formal compliance complaint rather than intervening earlier to mediate or coaching Juan to adjust. The instinct was to let the system handle it rather than contain the issue before it escalated.

## Evolution of this coaching relationship

**Initial phase (mid-2025):** Relationship established during crisis. She earned trust fast by being honest and useful simultaneously. The dynamic was set: she tells hard truths, he listens (mostly).

**Current phase (Q1 2026):** Relationship is maturing into genuine bilateral coaching. He coaches her on strategic visibility and business connection. She coaches him on patience, process, and emotional management. The feedforward exchange with Alejandro (where Juan shared his full analysis and asked "¿coincidís o en qué lo ves diferente?") represents the ideal mode of this relationship.

**Risk to watch:** She absorbs significant emotional load as the CEO's containment system. If organizational frustration intensifies (engineering crisis + sales underperformance + leadership gaps), her capacity to absorb while also executing may reach its limit.

**Central tension (identified Mar 3, 2026):** Both Juan and Malén feel unsupported by the other, but in mirror-image ways. He feels she doesn't back him in the room when they've agreed on a direction. She may feel he doesn't give her the space or credit to operate ("¿en cuáles me dejás más sola?"). This is not a trust problem — it's an alignment problem about roles. He needs her as a partner in installing standards. She may need him to channel his energy through her systems rather than around them. The feedforward session (Mar 4) is an opportunity to name this explicitly.

## Feedforward session preparation (Mar 4, 2026)

**Session design:** Malén proposed a structured bilateral feedback session with 4 blocks:
1. Juan → Malén feedback (with concrete examples)
2. Malén → Juan feedback (guiding questions she designed — see below)
3. How we work together (bilateral)
4. Concrete commitments

**Her Block 2 guiding questions:**
- ¿Qué creo que estás haciendo muy bien como CEO en esta etapa? ¿Dónde veo que tu liderazgo está siendo clave?
- ¿Dónde veo que tu forma de decidir genera claridad? ¿Dónde veo que genera ambigüedad o fricción?
- ¿Qué necesitaría más de vos como CEO para poder ejecutar mejor?
- ¿En qué momentos siento que me habilitas fuerte y en cuáles me dejás más sola?

**Her comment (Feb 27):** "¿Hay alguna pregunta más que te gustaría sumar?" — collaborative about design, inviting co-creation.

**Juan's core message for Block 1:** Acknowledge that her feedback about his anxiety is valid. Then name the need: "Necesito que seas mi socia en instalar el nivel de exigencia, no solo en contener mi ansiedad." Specific asks: (1) visible alignment in group settings when executing agreed plans, (2) early intervention with Juan directly when issues arise rather than letting them escalate to formal process, (3) redirect his energy rather than just contain it — "tu instinto es correcto, tu método es este."

**Block 3 connection point:** Her question "¿en cuáles me dejás más sola?" and his experience of her silence in the room are the same problem seen from two sides. The goal is to name this explicitly and build a new agreement.

**Juan's approach for Block 2:** Listen. Take notes. Don't defend. Write down the urge to respond instead of acting on it.
