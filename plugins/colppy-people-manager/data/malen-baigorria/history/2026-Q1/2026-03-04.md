## Incident

Malen sent a company-wide message (@channel) when she learned the office was unusable due to a fire in an extractor on a floor above, causing flooding. She told everyone not to come in, without consulting Juan first.

**Malen's message (8:26 AM):**
> @channel Buen dia equipo! Nos avisan del cowork que hubo un incendio en un extractor de un piso superior al nuestro, y eso causó una inundación en algunos pisos. No está utilizable la oficina asiq les pedimos que hoy no vengan. Los vamos a mantener al tanto por los próximos dias, estan trabajando en solucionar.

## Juan's feedback (8:51 AM)

Asked her to consult with him first before announcing to the company next time. The point: with such short notice, logistics could have been resolved differently (e.g., alternative workspace). The priority is the office and always will be — the question is how to facilitate people working together, not how to send everyone home. Asked her to keep this in mind for next time.

- **Her position:** "Yo no voy a poner en riesgo a la gente y a la compañia por garantizar presencialidad por un solo día."
- **Agreement:** "Mantengo mi decisión. Pero de acuerdo con avisarte a vos primero si existe una próxima vez."
- **Update:** Office supposedly to be habilitated in the afternoon; people can come normally tomorrow.

## Coaching signals

- **Process before consultation:** She acted unilaterally on a company-wide decision without consulting the CEO first. This connects to the coaching theme: early intervention with Juan before acting on major decisions.
- **She held her ground on substance:** Insurance and safety were valid reasons. She did not back down on the decision itself.
- **She agreed on process:** Committed to consulting him first if there is a next time — receptive to the feedback on *how*, not defensive about *what*.
- **Presencialidad vs. logistics:** Juan's point was about exploring alternatives (other spaces, logistics) before defaulting to "everyone stay home." Her response focused on safety and legal coverage — both valid, but the consultation would have allowed them to weigh options together.

## Connection to coaching arc

- **Early intervention vs. escalation:** She escalated to the whole company before aligning with Juan. The ask has been: come to Juan first on major decisions, not to cover things up, but so they can course-correct together.
- **Partner in standards:** Major operational decisions (office closure, company-wide communication) should be co-decided, not unilateral.
- **Positive:** She received the feedback without defensiveness on the process point and committed to consulting first next time.
