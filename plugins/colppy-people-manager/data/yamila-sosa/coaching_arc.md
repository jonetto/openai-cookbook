# Yamila Sosa — Coaching Arc
_Last updated: 2026-03-11_

## Development trajectory

- **Pre-Q3 2025**: Customer team operator. Deep Sueldos product knowledge. Execution-oriented, client-facing. Not at leadership table.
- **Q3 2025**: Transition to leadership table. First exposure to strategic conversations. Learning the difference between operating and leading.
- **Q4 2025**: Formally designated Sueldos product owner (Dec 19). Began strategic planning work. Started engaging with cross-functional initiatives (ebook, campaigns, ICPs). Feedforward evaluation: "cumple parcialmente" on objectives, "parcialmente alineado" on competencies. Core gap identified: operational posture in a strategic role.
- **Q1 2026 (current)**: Persistent struggle to complete strategic deliverables (Plan de Sueldos 2026 overdue since Jan 9). Sales results improving (23/36 products, MRR targets hit on high-ticket deals). However, fundamental ownership gaps exposed (campaign without tracking, pipeline deals not registered). "Role not meeting expectations" conversation (Mar 10). AI agent development path initiated (Mar 11).

## Coaching approaches tried

**1. Direct feedback in 1:1s (Nov 2025 → ongoing)**
First instance: Nov 5 Weekly Sueldos — "bring metrics, not task lists." Repeated in Dec 30 (strategic levers), Jan 28 (plan follow-up), Feb 4 (feedforward), Mar 10 (role expectations). **Result: message received but not absorbed into behavior.** The same coaching has been given 5+ times over 4 months. She is receptive in the moment but does not translate to changed operating rhythm.

**2. Framework provision (Dec 2025 → ongoing)**
Provided JTBD framework, palancas → iniciativas → KRs methodology, ICP simplification, strategic levers model. Juan did the strategic framing work and handed it to Yamila to execute. **Result: she accepts frameworks but does not produce them independently.** When Juan stops providing structure, she reverts to operational updates.

**3. Formal feedforward with structured recognitions + development areas (Feb 4, 2026)**
Bilateral session with 3 recognitions (client orientation, collaboration, dedication) and 3 development areas (structure, data-driven, strategic vs. operative). Yamila made 3 commitments (Jira system, strategic plan, change weekly dynamic). **Result: commitments largely unfulfilled as of Mar 10.** Plan still incomplete, no Jira implementation visible, weekly dynamic unchanged until Juan proposed removing her from Monday meetings.

**4. Role clarification through group process (Dec 19, 2025)**
At planning presencial, team formally assigned Yamila as Sueldos owner. **Result: title accepted but ownership behaviors did not follow.** Three months later, campaign tracking absent, pipeline visibility broken, strategic plan undelivered.

**5. Heavy enablement investment (Mar 11, 2026)**
2-hour hands-on session setting up Claude Desktop, VS Code, GitHub, connectors. Juan shared personal profile, demonstrated agent capabilities, proposed agent architecture, committed $200/month budget. **Result: too early to evaluate.** Risk is that this becomes another tool provided by Juan that Yamila doesn't sustain independently.

**6. Schedule restructuring proposal (Mar 10, 2026)**
Juan proposed removing Yamila from Monday meetings (~5 hours/week freed) to focus on higher-impact work. **Result: pending implementation.** If she uses the freed time strategically, this could be a positive structural intervention.

## Breakthrough moments

**None clearly documented yet.** There are positive operational moments:
- Cross-sell improvement in Feb/Mar (two high-ticket deals hitting MRR target)
- Multi-channel activation workflow design (WhatsApp + email + push + chat)
- ICP documentation completion (Dec 2025)
- Willing engagement with AI agent training (Mar 11)

But these are execution wins, not leadership breakthroughs. A breakthrough would be: Yamila bringing a data-driven analysis that changes a strategic decision, or proposing a structural fix to a systemic problem, or delivering the strategic plan without being asked. This has not happened yet.

## Regression patterns

**1. Task-list reporting (persistent since Nov 2025)**
Every time coaching redirects her toward strategic metrics, she reverts to operational task lists within 1-2 sessions. The pattern: Juan coaches → she agrees → next meeting she brings another task list. Five documented instances.

**2. Started-not-finished initiatives**
Ebook with marketing (mentioned Nov, still pending Dec, disappeared). 31-user call campaign (Dec, no follow-up). Plan de Sueldos 2026 (due Jan 9, still incomplete Mar 10). This is the core feedforward "regalo" — projects not closed end-to-end.

**3. Invisible ownership**
Being formally designated "owner" does not translate to ownership behavior. Campaign without tracking for months. Pipeline deals not registered. She does not monitor the things a product owner should monitor. Problems are discovered by others (Juan, Daniel the consultant) rather than surfaced by her.

**4. Emotional framing over analytical framing**
Surfaces frustration ("feeling stuck despite defending the product") rather than data-driven problem statements ("activation dropped from 15% to 5%, root cause is X, proposal is Y"). Energy is genuine but misdirected.

## Evolution of this coaching relationship

**Nov-Dec 2025: Establishing expectations.** First coaching interventions focused on the operational-to-strategic shift. Tone was instructional — Juan providing frameworks and redirecting from task lists to metrics. Implicit assumption that providing the right tools would enable the transition.

**Jan 2026: Growing concern.** Plan de Sueldos 2026 overdue. UTM misconfigurations discovered. WhatsApp campaign not creating contacts. Coaching became more prescriptive (specific deadlines, specific formats) but accountability loop still absent.

**Feb 2026: Formal feedforward.** First structured bilateral session with documented strengths and development areas. Yamila made specific commitments. This was the most structured coaching intervention to date.

**Mar 2026: Escalation.** "Role not meeting expectations" — first time Juan communicated this directly. Shift from patient development to explicit performance concern. Simultaneously, heavy investment in AI enablement (2-hour setup session). Tension between "you're not meeting the bar" and "here's a massive new tool to learn."

**Current coaching risk:** The relationship may be in a pattern where Juan oscillates between patient investment and frustrated escalation, without a clear accountability framework in between. The Mar 10 conversation was the right escalation, but needs to be followed by milestones with consequences, not another cycle of additive coaching.
